The same routine does not work all year, or in every city. A cream that is perfect in Seoul in summer can suffocate your skin in Paris in winter, or the other way around. The reason is simple: your skin reacts to its environment, and air humidity, temperature and UV change what it needs. Most routines ignore this. Here is how to adjust, without buying ten new products.

## The three variables that matter

**Air humidity.** When the air is dry (winter, heating, air conditioning, planes), it pulls water out of your skin. It feels tight, flaky, rough. When the air is humid, skin holds its water better, but a cream that is too rich can feel heavy and add shine.

**Temperature.** Cold weakens the skin barrier and slows renewal. Heat increases oil and sweat, so you need lighter textures.

**UV.** It is the leading cause of visible ageing and dark spots. Its intensity changes with season, weather and city, but it is present even on cloudy days.

## Winter and dry air: repair and retain

When it is cold and dry, the goal is to strengthen the barrier and hold water in.

- Lean on **humectants** (hyaluronic acid, glycerin) to bring water in, then seal with a richer cream containing **ceramides** and **niacinamide**.
- The sandwich technique helps: a hydrating serum on still-damp skin, then the occlusive on top.
- The trap: over-exfoliating. In cold weather the barrier is already fragile. Keep acids to two or three times a week.

## Summer, heat and humidity: lighten and protect

When it is hot and humid, skin holds its water more easily.

- Switch to **light** textures (gels, fluids) to avoid heaviness and excess shine.
- In the morning, an **antioxidant** like vitamin C helps neutralise the oxidative stress from UV and pollution.
- Sun protection is not optional: it is the most cost-effective anti-ageing step there is. If UV is high, apply more and reapply during the day.

## High UV: the rule that never changes

Whatever the season, as soon as the UV index climbs, two steps come first: **antioxidant in the morning** and **stronger sun protection**. That is true in Paris in spring as much as in Seoul in summer.

## How to adjust without rebuying everything

You do not need a wardrobe of products. Keep a stable core (gentle cleanser, one targeted active, a cream, an SPF) and **adjust one or two steps per season**:

- in winter, add an occlusive and space out acids;
- in summer, lighten the cream and step up the SPF;
- all year, keep the antioxidant if your UV is high.

## In short

Your skin does not live in a lab, it lives in your city. The right move is not to follow a fixed routine, but to adjust to the climate and season your skin is actually going through.
