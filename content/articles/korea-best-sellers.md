## What Olive Young is (and why it sets the standard)

When people talk about "what works in Korea," one retailer is the compass: **Olive Young**. Founded in Seoul in 1999, it is by far Korea's number-one beauty retailer: more than **1,380 stores**, **over 85% of the health-and-beauty store market**, and around **$4.2 billion** in 2025 revenue (5.83 trillion won, up 21.8% year on year).

Why it matters to you: Olive Young's charts and annual **Awards** are built on the **real sales** of millions of Koreans, widely seen as some of the most demanding skincare shoppers in the world. These aren't influencer picks, they're sales data. And the trends usually reach the West **six to twelve months later**, so following them puts you ahead.

## The trends rising in 2026

According to Olive Young data, three signals stand out this year:

- **Niacinamide**: searches up roughly **26%** year on year, the most in-demand glow/oil-control active.
- **PDRN** (from salmon DNA): **+90%**, the new repair star.
- **"Capsule" creams and gel-creams**: searches surging (up to +1,379% for "capsule cream"), a shift toward next-generation hydrating textures.
